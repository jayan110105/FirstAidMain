Poisoning First Aid

Poisoning occurs when a person ingests, inhales, or comes into contact with toxic substances...

Signs and Symptoms of Poisoning

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest pain or tightness
  • Nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
  • Excessive salivation or drooling
  • Drowsiness, confusion, or dizziness
  • Seizures
  • Burns or redness around the mouth or skin (if the poison is corrosive)
  • Abnormal pupil size (dilated or constricted)

First Aid for Poisoning

1. Ingestion Poisoning

  • Call for help via the emergency helpline number
  • DO NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by a medical professional
  • If the person is conscious, offer them water or milk (only if instructed by a medical professional)
  • Identify the poison if possible (bring the container, plant, or substance involved)

2. Inhalation Poisoning

  • Get the person to fresh air immediately while keeping yourself safe from exposure
  • If the person is unconscious but breathing, place them in the recovery position to ensure their airway remains clear
  • Call emergency services immediately

3. Contact Poisoning

  • Wash the affected skin immediately with soap and water
  • If the poison is from a plant (e.g., poison ivy), wash the affected area and avoid scratching
  • For chemical burns, flush the area with large amounts of water for at least 20 minutes
  • Seek medical attention if severe reactions occur

4. Injection Poisoning

  • Call emergency services immediately
  • If the substance is known (e.g., heroin), administer Naloxone (Narcan) if available
  • For stings or bites, remove the stinger or needle carefully without squeezing
  • Monitor vital signs (breathing, pulse) and be prepared to administer CPR if necessary

When to Seek Medical Help

  • If the person is unconscious, drowsy, or confused
  • If the person cannot breathe or is showing difficulty breathing
  • If the person shows signs of severe poisoning (e.g., seizures, paralysis, severe burns)
  • If the poisoning involves a child or elderly person
  • If you cannot identify the poison
